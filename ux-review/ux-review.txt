BRUTAL UX REVIEW: 55-YEAR-OLD TRUCK DRIVER ON MOBILE
=====================================================
Date: 2026-03-16
Reviewer: Claude (Anthropic)
Scope: All 9 form steps in /src/components


1. THE LABELS ARE NEARLY INVISIBLE
-----------------------------------
This is the single biggest problem in the entire app.

Every label in FormElements.tsx:17 is:
  text-[10px] uppercase tracking-widest font-bold text-slate-400

10 pixels. Light gray. All caps. Stretched letter-spacing. On a phone screen,
those labels are nearly unreadable for anyone, let alone someone who might wear
reading glasses. A 55-year-old squinting at their phone in a truck stop parking
lot has zero chance. This applies to every single field in the entire form.


2. ZERO REQUIRED FIELD INDICATORS
-----------------------------------
Not a single field is marked as required. The driver has no idea which fields
they can skip and which will block them from submitting. They'll fill out the
whole thing, hit submit, and then get a wall of red errors they have to hunt for.


3. THE DRIVING EXPERIENCE TABLE WILL BREAK ON MOBILE
-----------------------------------------------------
Steps3-4.tsx:32 — A <table> with three columns rendered inside overflow-x-auto.
On a phone, the user has to horizontally scroll a table while filling out a
multi-step form on a page that also scrolls vertically. This is one of the most
confusing mobile UI patterns that exists. The radio buttons inside are labeled
just "Y" and "N" — not "Yes" and "No". A confused driver will tap Y/N, the page
will scroll sideways, and they'll lose their place.


4. THE FORM STARTS EMPTY ON CRITICAL STEPS
-------------------------------------------
Steps 4, 5, and 6 (License Details, Employment, Training) all start completely
blank with just a small "+ Add License / + Add Employer" button in the corner.
A non-tech user will stare at a white screen and think the page didn't load.
There is no instruction that says "Click the button above to add your first
license." The button is small and positioned top-right where it blends into
the header.


5. JARGON EVERYWHERE, NO EXPLANATIONS
---------------------------------------
This form is aimed at truck drivers, not lawyers. Here's what is unexplained:

- TWIC card — no explanation. Port entry card. Many drivers have never needed one.
- FMCSR — step title says "Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations" in the
  subtitle, but that's 8px gray text most will skip.
- DD214 — military discharge document. No hint for veterans who may not
  remember the term.
- FMCSR / TC safety regs — "TC" is Transport Canada. Not explained anywhere.
- PSP Authorization — no explanation at all. Just a link and a checkbox.
- 49 C.F.R. PART 391.23 — a legal code citation as a section header. Means
  nothing to a driver.
- X Endorsement — no explanation. It's a combo HazMat + Tanker endorsement.
- SSN / SIN — SIN is the Canadian Social Insurance Number. A US driver will
  wonder if they're being asked about a criminal record.


6. "DRAG TO UPLOAD" ON A PHONE
--------------------------------
Step9-Documents.tsx:52 — "Click or drag to upload"

You cannot drag files on a mobile phone. This instruction actively confuses
mobile users. They'll try to "drag" something and nothing will happen.


7. SCROLLABLE LEGAL TEXT INSIDE A SCROLLABLE PAGE
---------------------------------------------------
Steps7-8.tsx:148 — max-h-40 overflow-y-auto

There are two scrollable legal text boxes embedded in a page the user is
already scrolling. On mobile, when your finger hits one of these boxes, you'll
scroll the inner box, not the page. The user won't know why the page stopped
scrolling. They'll think the app froze. The text inside is also text-[11px]
italic light gray — illegible under any reasonable mobile conditions.


8. PSP AUTHORIZATION HAS NO ACKNOWLEDGMENT CHECKBOX
-----------------------------------------------------
Steps7-8.tsx:139-144 — The PSP section has a heading and a link but no
checkbox. Every other section has one. This looks like an accidental omission
and creates a legal gap.


9. THE PREVIOUS EMPLOYMENT SECTION REQUIRES AN ADDRESS FOR EVERY JOB
----------------------------------------------------------------------
For each employer: company name, street address, city, state, zip, country,
phone, position, reason for leaving — plus 4 Yes/No questions. Most 55-year-old
drivers won't remember the street address of a trucking company they left 6
years ago. There's no hint like "Look this up on Google Maps" or "Approximate
address is fine." They'll either quit the form or leave it blank and get
an error.


10. THE DOT NOTICE IS WRITTEN IN BUREAUCRATIC MATH
----------------------------------------------------
Steps5-6.tsx:17-20:
  "...for the past 3 years. For the 7 years preceding that 3 years..."

Read that out loud. "The 7 years preceding that 3 years" means go back 10 years
total. Say that instead: "up to 10 years of employment history." A driver
reading this on a phone at 7am before a shift will reread it three times, shrug,
and guess.


11. THE PREVIOUS WORK DETAILS FIELD IS ONE BOX FOR FOUR THINGS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Steps1-2.tsx:161:
  label="Enter start/end dates, location, position, and reason for leaving"
  placeholder="Dates: 2020-2022, Location: Chicago, Position: Driver, Reason: Relocation"

This is a single <Input> (one line text box) asking for 4 pieces of structured
data. The placeholder disappears the moment they tap it. They'll type "2019"
and not know what else to add.


12. SSN FIELD HAS NO MASKING
------------------------------
Steps1-2.tsx:49 — SSN is a plain text input. On mobile, the keyboard that opens
is a standard keyboard — not a numeric keypad. There's no masking (it won't
format as XXX-XX-XXXX while they type). Older users are also anxious about
typing their SSN into a phone app — a small "Why do we need this?" tooltip
would go a long way.


13. SELECT DROPDOWNS HAVE NO VISIBLE ARROW
-------------------------------------------
FormElements.tsx:48 — appearance-none

The native dropdown arrow is removed with appearance-none, and there's no
custom chevron added. The field looks identical to a text input. A non-tech
user won't know it's tappable or that it has options.


14. THE UNEMPLOYMENT SECTION FEELS ACCUSATORY
-----------------------------------------------
Steps5-6.tsx:182:
  "Have you been unemployed at any time within the last 3 years?"

With no explanation of why this is being asked, a driver who took a month off
between jobs might feel like they're being accused of something. A single
sentence — "This is required by DOT regulations" — would dramatically reduce
anxiety here.


15. NO SAVE AND RESUME
-----------------------
This is a 9-step form that will take 30-45 minutes to complete. There is no
visible save functionality. If the driver gets a call, loses connection, or
their phone locks up, they start over. That is a form abandonment guaranteed.

--------

- Mark required fields clearly
- Replace the driving experience table with stacked mobile cards
- Add "Save progress" / resume later

- Fix "drag to upload" copy on mobile
- Add plain-English explanations for TWIC, FMCSR, PSP, DD214
- Split the previous-work-details single field into proper structured inputs
- Add SSN numeric keypad and masking
- Fix the scrollable-box-inside-scrollable-page legal sections

MEDIUM
------

- Add empty-state guidance on License/Employment steps
- Add a PSP acknowledgment checkbox
